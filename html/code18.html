Code 18: <q> Inline Quote

The <q> element represents a short inline quotation. Browsers typically add quotation marks automatically.

What it does

<q> is for short quotes that sit inside a sentence or paragraph. It tells the browser "this is quoted speech".

Unlike typing quotes yourself, <q> is semantic — search engines and screen readers know it's a quote.


The Code

HTML 
<p>When she said <q>HTML is fun</q>, I had to agree.</p>
<p>The teacher told us <q cite="https://developer.mozilla.org">Always close your tags</q> on day one.</p>

Live Example

When she said HTML is fun, I had to agree.

The teacher told us Always close your tags on the first day.

He whispered Never gonna give you up during the meeting.


<q> vs <blockquote>

q vs blockquote: Use <q> for short quotes that sit inside a paragraph. Use <blockquote> for longer quotes that need their own block.

Don't add your own quotes — the browser does it for <q>.
